Ray Douglas Bradbury, 1920-2012

A great American writer and master of the short story.

If you're reluctant to weep, you won't live a full and complete life. If you're embarrassed because you have some notion about how men are supposed to behave and it doesn't include weeping, then you have some personal work to do.

-Ray Bradbury

A brief summary of an amazing life:

  • 1920 - Ray is born on August 22 in Waukegean, Illinois
  • 1932 - Young Ray meets a magician at a carnival, who commanded him to "Live Forever!" sparking his passion to write every day, a habit he kept his entire life
  • 1934 - The Bradbury family resettles in Los Angeles; Ray falls in love with Hollywood
  • 1936 - Ray joins the Los Angeles Science Fiction Society and begins join weekly meetings
  • 1938 - Ray publishes his first story, is rejected from service in WWII due to poor eyesight
  • 1947 - Ray publishes Dark Carnival his first collection of short stories
  • 1953 - Ray publishes Farenheit 451, a landmark novel in his career
  • 1964 - Ray consults on design of the American Pavilion at the 1964 New York World's Fair and writes the narration script for the American Journey attraction
  • 1980's - Ray hosts The Ray Bradbury Theater a serialized tevelsion show based on his short stories
  • 1982 - "People ask me to predict the Future, when all I want to do is prevent it"

"Libraries raised me. I don't believe in colleges and universities. I believe in libraries because most students don't have any money. When I graduated from high school, it was during the Depression and we had no money. I couldn't go to college, so I went to the library three days a week for 10 years."

- Ray Bradbury
